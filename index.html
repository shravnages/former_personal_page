The Effects of the ATOH-1 Gene on the Different Transdifferentiation Process in Cilia

KiBow-Biotech R&D Labs (Pennsylvania, United States) — 2015

-Inspired by my fear of losing hearing as a drummer, I created an experiment to determine if a certain gene could reproduce stereocilia or inner ear hair cells, in humans, using an organism called Stentor as a model of the hair cells.
-Performed experiment with a team of researchers and mentors. Received statistically significant results and submitted findings in many research competitions, with great success in these events. Selected as one of three speakers to present in Imperial’s annual TEDx Autumn Series event.

Automating heart segmentation in post-processing of in vivo cardiac diffusion tensor imaging data

Royal Brompton Hospital (South Kensington, United Kingdom) — 2017 (summer)

-Aims: To automate segmentation of regions of interest and speed-up cardiac DTI analysis. This will minimise clinicians input time, and provide a consistent and reproducible segmentation by eliminating human subjectivity.
-Will perform under the guidance of Prof. David Firmin, Department of Physics, Imperial College London.
